What is Mounjaro (Tirzepatide)?

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is a breakthrough weekly injectable medication developed by Eli Lilly. It is the first and only FDA-approved dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist, meaning it activates two separate gut-hormone pathways simultaneously — making it significantly more powerful than single-action GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic.

In landmark clinical trials (SURMOUNT programme), patients on Mounjaro lost an average of 20–22% of their body weight over 72 weeks — the highest weight reduction ever recorded in a pharmaceutical trial. At DermaTales, we administer Mounjaro under strict physician supervision, with full monitoring and personalised dose titration for optimal safety and results.

How Mounjaro Works

Unlike traditional weight loss medications, Mounjaro operates via two complementary mechanisms that together produce far greater outcomes:

GLP-1 Receptor Activation

Reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying, and lowers blood sugar by stimulating insulin release in a glucose-dependent manner — preventing dangerous hypoglycaemia.

GIP Receptor Activation

Enhances fat metabolism directly in fat cells, reduces inflammatory signals, and amplifies the appetite-suppressing effects of GLP-1 — uniquely Mounjaro's advantage.

Visceral Fat Reduction

Clinical data confirms Mounjaro specifically reduces visceral (abdominal) fat — the most dangerous type linked to cardiovascular disease and metabolic syndrome.

Cardiometabolic Benefits

Significant improvements in blood pressure, cholesterol, HbA1c, and liver fat seen in trials — making Mounjaro uniquely beneficial for those with diabetes or metabolic syndrome.

Ideal Candidates

  • Adults with BMI ≥ 30, or ≥ 27 with a weight-related health condition.
  • People with Type 2 diabetes seeking simultaneous glucose and weight control.
  • Those who haven't achieved sufficient results with Ozempic or other GLP-1 drugs.
  • Patients with metabolic syndrome, PCOS, or high cardiovascular risk.

Contraindications

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC).
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2).
  • Pregnancy or planned pregnancy — must be stopped ≥2 months before conception.
  • Active or prior pancreatitis (relative contraindication, assessed case-by-case).

Mounjaro Treatment Protocol at DermaTales

Pre-Treatment Medical Screening

Full history, physical examination, and blood panel (thyroid, pancreatic enzymes, blood sugar, lipids, kidneys, liver) to confirm eligibility and establish a baseline.

Starting Dose: 2.5 mg Weekly

Mounjaro is always initiated at the lowest dose (2.5 mg SC weekly) for 4 weeks to allow your body to adapt and minimise gastrointestinal side effects.

Gradual Dose Escalation

Doses are increased every 4 weeks (2.5 → 5 → 7.5 → 10 → 12.5 → 15 mg) based on tolerance and clinical response, up to the therapeutic target dose.

Monthly Follow-Up & Monitoring

Regular clinic visits to monitor weight, body composition, blood markers, and side effects, with dietary and lifestyle coaching at every visit.

Maintenance & Transition Planning

After reaching goal weight, a structured weaning or long-term maintenance protocol is designed to preserve your results and support physiological adaptation.
