Types of Chemical Peels We Offer

We offer a full spectrum of certified medical-grade peels, each targeting specific skin issues with precision:

Glycolic Acid Peel

An AHA-based superficial peel ideal for brightening dull skin, improving texture, and addressing mild pigmentation and fine lines.

Salicylic Acid Peel

A BHA-based peel that deeply penetrates pores, making it the gold standard for oily, acne-prone skin. Effectively reduces blackheads and active breakouts.

TCA (Trichloroacetic Acid) Peel

A medium-depth peel for significant pigmentation, deep acne scars, and skin laxity. Stimulates substantial collagen remodelling for a smoother surface.

VI Peel / Combination Peel

A sophisticated blend of acids (TCA, salicylic, retinoic acid) suitable for all skin types. Targets melasma, hyperpigmentation, and moderate photo-ageing with minimal downtime.

Mandelic Acid Peel

A gentle AHA with anti-bacterial properties, perfect for sensitive and darker skin tones. Effective for mild acne, pigmentation, and improved skin clarity.

Lactic Acid / Kojic Peel

Specifically targets uneven skin tone, melasma, and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH). Gentle, hydrating, and safe for most skin types.

How Chemical Peels Work

A chemical peel works by applying a carefully selected acid solution to the skin, which causes the controlled exfoliation and eventual shedding of the outermost layers. As the skin peels, fresh, new cells generate from beneath, revealing a smoother, more even, and radiant complexion. Simultaneously, the controlled injury triggers collagen production in the dermis — the key to long-term improvements in skin texture, firmness, and tone.

Even Skin Tone

Anti-Ageing

Stimulates collagen to reduce fine lines, improve skin elasticity, and restore a youthful appearance.

Minimal Downtime

Superficial and medium peels offer a quick recovery — most patients return to daily activities within 2–5 days.

Ideal Candidates

  • Individuals with acne, oily skin, or recurring breakouts.
  • Those with pigmentation, melasma, or sun damage.
  • Anyone seeking brighter, more even-toned skin.
  • People with mild-to-moderate signs of ageing.

Who Should Avoid

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women.
  • Active skin infections or open wounds.
  • Those on isotretinoin within the past 6 months.
  • Individuals with a history of hypertrophic scarring.

The DermaTales Procedure Steps

Expert Skin Analysis

Our dermatologist evaluates your skin type, Fitzpatrick scale, and specific concerns to prescribe the most effective peel formulation and depth.

Skin Priming (Pre-Peel)

A 2-week pre-peel regime may be advised using retinoids or hydroquinone to maximise results and reduce the risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

Cleansing & Degreasing

Your skin is thoroughly cleansed and degreased to ensure even penetration of the peel solution across all treatment areas.

Precision Peel Application

The selected acid solution is applied in a controlled manner. Contact time is carefully monitored for optimal depth and uniform effect.

Neutralisation & Soothing Care

The peel is neutralised where required, followed by application of calming serums, a barrier moisturiser, and broad-spectrum SPF.
